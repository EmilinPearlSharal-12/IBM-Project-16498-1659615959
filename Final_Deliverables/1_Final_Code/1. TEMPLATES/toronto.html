The University of Toronto is home to some of the most prestigious architecture and beautiful green spaces in the Greater Toronto Area and Mississauga. The U of T offers an extraordinary environment for students to learn and work. The University of Toronto (U of T) is segregated into three campuses, the University of Toronto Mississauga (UTM), the University of Toronto St. George campus and the University of Toronto Scarborough (UTSC). The campuses are located in the most vibrant urban environments that are dynamic, inviting areas with something for everyone. From sports teams to cultural happenings, festivals to street fairs, Toronto has it all. University of Toronto student enrolment (fall 2021-22) includes a total of 97,066 students studying at the University. Of which 75,582 applied for undergraduate programmes and 21,484 applied for graduate programmes. Of the total 97,066 students, 27,130 were international enrolments. The University of Toronto has more than 1,100 student clubs, organizations and activities across all three campuses. The University of Toronto also has a housing guarantee for full-time students entering their first year of university in an undergraduate program. The University of Toronto has 40 libraries and is home to the third-largest library system in North America. The University of Toronto (U of T) is a rich network of 640,000 alumni who are exploring new frontiers in their careers and communities and creating positive change all over the world. Description

The University of Toronto offers accommodation to its UG and PG students in all three of its campus i.e. St. George, Mississauga and Scarborough

Each campus has its own distinct residential communities and styles of residential living

U of T St. George residents live in one of the seven colleges, in Chestnut Residence, or in Student Family Housing

U of T Mississauga has more than 1,500 students living in residence and offers three different housing options: residence halls, townhouses or apartments

U of T Scarborough residents live less than a two-minute walk from academic buildings and the accommodations are either townhouses or apartment suites
