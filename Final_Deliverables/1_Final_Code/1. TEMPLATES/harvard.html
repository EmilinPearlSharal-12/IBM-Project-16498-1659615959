Harvard University

About

Established by means of a vote by the Great and General Court of the Massachusetts Bay Colony, Harvard - the first college in the US, was founded on September 08, 1636. The oldest institution of higher learning in the United States, Harvard University is a private Ivy League research university located in Cambridge, Massachusetts and is among the most prestigious universities in the world. When we talk about the Harvard University ranking, the University is among the best in the world. The US News & World Report - Global Universities ranking has ranked the university at the #1 spot for five years in a row (2018-2022). The US News & World Report - National University Ranking has ranked the university at the #2 spot for five years in a row (2018-2022). The ARWU (Shanghai Ranking) - Universities Rankings has also ranked the University at the #1 spot for four years in a row, (2018-2021). Popular ranking groups, QS - World University Ranking and THE (Times Higher Education) - University Ranking have also ranked the university at #5 and #2 spots for 2022, respectively. Harvard University has three main campuses, they are in Cambridge, Allston, and Boston which are home to students and faculty, an epicentre of research and innovation, and a destination for visitors from all over the world. The campus boasts of the oldest library system in the United States that supports students and the public with vast archives and pioneering technologies. The campus also has museums that feature unique and varied collections, with educational knowledge for everyone. A statue of John Harvard – the first major benefactor who donated half of his estate and his library of over 400 books to the School, stands in front of University Hall in Harvard Yard and is perhaps the University’s best-known landmark. Harvard University consists of the undergraduate Harvard College, the Harvard Radcliffe Institute and 12 graduate and professional schools. Harvard College is a diverse, close-knit undergraduate community of students who pursue academic concentrations and reimagine the future. The 12 graduate schools at Harvard University include Harvard John A. Paulson School of Engineering and Applied Sciences, Harvard Graduate School of Arts and Sciences, Harvard Extension School, Harvard Faculty of Arts and Sciences, Harvard Business School, Harvard School of Dental Medicine, Harvard Graduate School of Design, Harvard Divinity School, Harvard Graduate School of Education, Harvard Kennedy School, Harvard Law School, Harvard Medical School, Harvard T.H. Chan School of Public Health. The Harvard Radcliffe Institute is a laboratory of ideas that brings together a diversity of voices to engage with issues that can only be fully understood with cross-disciplinary exploration. Harvard has a diverse student population from over 50 states and 80 countries and for Fall 2021, there are 25,510 students studying at Harvard University out of which 7,095 students are enrolled at Harvard College and 18,415 students are pursuing graduate and professional studies. When we talk about the Class of 2025, a total of 57,786 applicants applied to Harvard College and 2,320 students were admitted to the various programmes at the University. Around 15.6% of the applicants were international candidates. Read: Harvard University Admissions The University offers both need-based and merit-based scholarships in terms of financial aid, and also has separate course-specific aids available for both UG and PG aspirants. The university encourages prospective students to use their Net Price Calculator which provides an estimate of how much they need to invest after availing of financial aid. It does not guarantee financial aid but only states an estimate. According to Harvard, 100% of UG applicants with scholarships can graduate without any debts. Please note that each financial aid program has its separate deadline, so do check their respective website for details on the process and deadlines. When we talk about university accommodation, first-year students from Harvard College live in dorms with shared suites adjacent to Harvard Yard and dine at Annenberg Hall. At the end of their first year, students are allotted one of Harvard's 12 Houses. Each House accommodates between 350 and 500 students and helps create a close-knit community within the University walls. There are more than 400,000 Harvard alumni around the world spread across over 200+ countries. The university is home to 2,400 world-class faculty members and more than 10,000 academic appointments in affiliated teaching hospitals. Harvard University Alumni have 78+ Nobel Laureates, 132+ Pulitzer Prize Winners and 17+ Royalty and Nobility who have been part of the Harvard alumni. Description

The housing system at Harvard is designed to create a full collegiate experience for all four years of undergraduate education

As freshmen, students live in one of the dormitories in Harvard Yard, a prime location, and eat in the historic and picturesque Annenberg dining hall

After their first year at Harvard, students are placed into one of the 12 houses on campus and continue to live there for the remainder of their residential life at Harvard

A 13th House, Dudley House, serves non-residents and includes graduate student members

Each House accommodates 350-500 students with its own dining hall, common rooms, and facilities for academic, recreational, and cultural activities
