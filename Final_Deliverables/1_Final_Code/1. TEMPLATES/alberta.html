University of Alberta

About

The University of Alberta in Edmonton is one of the top teaching and research universities situated in Canada. Established in the year 1908, this public research university offers more than 200 undergraduate programs and 500 graduate programs. UAlberta is one of the top 5 research universities in Canada. The majority of the work at The University of Alberta is driven by the pursuit of truth. Using some of the world’s most advanced research facilities and resources, the researchers at Ualberta have discovered new knowledge in health and life sciences, energy, artificial intelligence, etc. If we talk about the rankings then, it is ranked at 126th position as per QS World Universities Rankings 2022 and 125th in the university rankings by the Times Higher Education 2022. There are total 5 University of Alberta campuses situated in 2 cities. The campuses situated in Edmonton are known by the names; North Campus, Campus-Saint Jean, South Campus, and Enterprise Square. The Augustana campus is situated in Camrose. The North campus and south campus provide facilities like the physical activity and wellness centre, gym, lifestyle centre, tennis court, etc. There are 40,061 students from 156 countries enrolled at The University of Alberta. The distinctive qualities of Uof Alberta include the faculty of native studies, library, and its alumni. The Faculty of Native Studies is the only faculty of its kind in Canada that provides undergraduate programs specializing in issues of language, culture, land, and resources. Apart from this, the library of Uof Alberta is Canada’s second-largest research library. It contains more than 4.7 million titles, 8.7 million volumes, 1.3 million e-books, and 1,700 databases. The University of Alberta’s graduates ranked at 2nd position in Canada for employment rate. This is because of the multiple programs for internships, job shadowing, and career mentoring provided at Uof Alberta. Various student opportunities are also provided at The University of Alberta. This includes the 425 student groups that provide students opportunities to pursue, academic, recreational, and political interests. Aside from this, students can also study at partner institutions around the world, including the Worldwide Universities Network, a consortium of 23 top research universities. At present, more than 300,000 alumni in 140 countries carry on the University of Alberta’s vision to serve the public good. Collectively, the alumni at the University of Alberta have founded more than 70,000 organizations globally. The notable alumni include Margaret Atwood (poet, novelist, essayist, literary critic), Joe Clark (former prime minister of Canada), Beverly McLachlin (former chief justice of Canada), Richard Taylor (Nobel Laureate in Physics), Violet King Henry (First black woman lawyer in Canada).
