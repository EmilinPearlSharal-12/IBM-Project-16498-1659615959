The University of British Columbia

About

The University of British Columbia was established in 1908 and opened in 1915. It is a public university in the Canadian state of British Columbia. The University of British Columbia (UBC) is a centre for teaching, learning and research excellence. With the motto, Tuum Est meaning “It is Yours” the UBC is a declaration of its drive to develop opportunities for people who see a better world for themselves and others. The University of British Columbia is one of the top 20 public universities in the world. The QS World University Ranking 2023, ranked the University of British Columbia #47 in the overall world university rankings. The Times Higher Education (THE) ranked UBC #37 in the overall world university rankings and #13 in the Impact rankings for the year 2022. Meanwhile, US News & World Report ranked the University of British Columbia #35 in the category of Best Global Universities for the year 2022. The first campus of the University of British Columbia was built in Vancouver. In the year 2005, the second campus of the University of British Columbia was opened in Okanagan, Kelowna. UBC Robson Square – Extension of the UBC Vancouver campus, the UBC Learning Exchange and UBC’s Centre for Digital Media are in Vancouver. The University of British Columbia offers 254 undergraduate programs and 244 postgraduate programs in the field of Business, Science, Education, Health and Medicine, Engineering, and Humanities. UBC provides clinical education to Faculty of Medicine students at 80+ training sites across British Columbia. With an annual consolidated budget of approximately $3.4 billion, UBC spends $759 million on research funding for more than ten thousand projects. UBC has two regional bases in Hong Kong and India to make local connections and elevate partnerships for research, learning and capacity development. There are more than 58,000 students at the Vancouver campus and 11,000+ students at the Okanagan campus of the University of British Columbia, in the academic year of 2021/2022. On the Okanagan campus, 21.8% of the total students are international students, while, 28.6% of the students at the Vancouver campus are international students. The Vancouver campus provides residences to more than 13,000 students in 15 residences. Most of the students qualify for the First Year Guarantee in winter session residence. First-year students are assigned to live only in Orchard Commons, Place Vanier or Totem Park residence and students are required to purchase a residence meal plan living in these residences. The rest of the residences are for Upper year and graduate students. The typical cost for a room and meal plan for a new undergraduate student is roughly $12,000-$14,500 per year. However, depending on the room type, fees can range from $10,300-$15,000 per year. The University of British Columbia is one of the finest universities in the world. UBC has produced, 8 Nobel laureates, 3 Canadian Prime Ministers, 65 Olympics Medal including 19 gold medals, by varsity athletes, and 74 Rhodes Scholars.
